Oyster

Where to find

The Oyster is a common sight in many temperate forests, often growing out of the side of trees in large, fan-like caps. They can also be sold in small clusters of caps in supermarkets. Be careful picking them wild, as they have a toxic lookalike in Australia and Japan. Fun fact: it's one of the only carnivorous mushrooms, as its mycelia can kill and digest nematodes.

How to cook the Oyster Mushroom

With a taste that's described as being similar to anise, with mild notes of nuts and earth, it has found its niche in Asian cooking, often in soups, stir-fries, or on its own. The oyster is best picked and used young, as it gets tough, and the flavor becomes unpleasant and bitter the longer it ages.
