Portabella

Where to find

It's hard not to find a portobello mushroom, as it's one of the most commonly sold fungus in the world, though it is native to the grasslands of Europe and North America. While immature, it's sold as a common white, or button, mushroom. It only becomes a true porto when the cap opens and you can see the gills. Even then, it can be sold under names like baby bella, Roman brown, cremini, or chestnut. This is the typical mushroom that everyone has eaten or at least seen.

How to cook the bella

This mushroom can do it all. It's a jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none deal with this one, being tasty and inoffensive to appeal to a huge mainstream audience. Since it comes in many forms, I'll stick to the basic Portobello mushroom. This guy's a really meaty mushroom. With a nice, mild, earthy flavor, and giant size, up to 6 inches across, you can easily use these as hamburger replacements, or as bun replacements. They can go anywhere: salads, pizza, pasta, sandwiches, sauteed in butter or breaded and fried in oil, this is the go-to mushroom for millions.
