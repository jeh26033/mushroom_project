The Chanterelle

Where to find

The Chanterelle is a golden/orange mushroom found commonly in northern parts of North America, Europe, Central America, and Asia. They like mossy coniferous forests, but can also be found around birch trees. Interestingly, they contain massive amounts of potassium and niacin, and are one of the richest known sources of Vitamin D.

This is a mushroom with class, being popularized by French chefs in the 18th century when it was served to nobility. Nowadays, it's a very common sight in Europe and North American kitchens. The flavor is hard to nail down, with some describing a woody, earthy flavor, while others taste nuts and fruits. The flavor comes down to location and conditions in which it was grown.

Best ways to cook a Chanterelle

Most of the flavor compounds in Chanterelles are fat-soluble, which makes them great for sauteing in butter or oil. They also go very well with wine, due to low moisture content. Consider them go to's for things like cream sauces and soups. They also dry very well.
