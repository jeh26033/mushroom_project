Shitake

Where to find

The Shitake is found natively in east Asia, where it is prized for its medicinal benefits. It is found in rotting tree trunks in deciduous forests. It can also be found in the humid climates of southeast Asia. The name comes from the shii tree, on which this mushroom is known to commonly grow. It is also known as the sawtooth mushroom, black mushroom, or oakwood mushroom. Shitakes make up 25% of yearly mushroom production worldwide.

How to cook Shitake

Due to their high moisture content (90%), they are often used in soups like miso, or vegetarian Dashi, and form the basis of many other steamed or simmered dishes. Shitakes are described as having a very rich smokey flavor, with a nice texture, with about 10 times the mushroom flavor of the typical white button mushroom. These also dry nicely, which concentrates the flavor even more.
